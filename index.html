About the tool

What is ToxRTool?

ToxRTool (Toxicological data Reliability assessment Tool) was developed by the European Union Reference Laboratory for alternatives to animal testing (EURL ECVAM) at the Joint Research Centre of the European Commission. It provides a structured, transparent way to evaluate the inherent quality of toxicological studies.

Reference: Schneider K. et al. (2009). ToxRTool, a new tool to assess the reliability of toxicological data. Regulatory Toxicology and Pharmacology, 54(2), 162–173.

How it works

Three steps to a reliability score

Complete an assessment in minutes. Export results as Excel or PDF for your dossier.

📋

Select study type & enter info

Choose between an in vivo or in vitro study. Enter authors, title, facility, and your evaluator details.

⚖️

Score each criterion

Work through all criteria grouped into five logical sections. Toggle 0 or 1 per criterion. Mandatory criteria (shown in red) must be met for Categories 1 or 2.

📤

Review & export

The tool calculates Categories A and B automatically. Confirm your final proposal (C) and export as a fully populated Excel file or print-ready report.

Scoring system

Reliability categories

Derived from the Klimisch et al. classification, adapted by EURL ECVAM for systematic use.

Category Label In vivo score In vitro score Typical use
Cat. 1 Reliable without restrictions 18–21 pts + all ● met 15–18 pts + all ● met Key study; check relevance
Cat. 2 Reliable with restrictions 13–17 pts + all ● met 11–14 pts + all ● met Potentially useful; check relevance
Cat. 3 Not reliable <13 pts or ● not met <11 pts or ● not met Generally not as key study
Cat. 4 Not assignable Documentation insufficient (reviews, handbooks, etc.) Not for key study use

● = mandatory criterion. If any mandatory criterion scores 0, maximum assignable category is 3, regardless of total score.
